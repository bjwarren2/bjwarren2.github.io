Research Assistant/Field Technician

  • Summer Employment
  • J.C. Raulston Arboretum
  • Raleigh, NC
  • July 2014 - August 2014

As a Research Assistant/Field Technician at J.C. Raulston Arboretum, I was responsible for coordinating sample tagging and harvesting research studies, as well as ensuring that precise size standards of 50 flowering plants were met in NCSU controlled environments, and organized field cuttings while accurately mapping plantings. Additionally, assisted with research projects within the Department of Horticulture and NCSU greenhouses. My attention to detail and organizational skills were crucial to ensuring the success of these research projects. By effectively coordinating and maintaining the necessary protocols, I helped to ensure that the research was carried out accurately and efficiently. My experience in research and plant cultivation made me a valuable asset to the team, and my contribution was essential to the overall success of the studies done.

   

From this experience, I acquired a variety of valuable skills, including: 

Research skills: My experience as a Research Assistant/Field Technician helped medevelop strong research skills, including data collection and analysis, and the ability to follow established protocols. 

Attention to detail: My responsibilities for ensuring precise size standards and accurately mapping plantings helped me develop a keen eye for detail and a strong focus on accuracy. 

Organizational skills: Coordinating sample tagging and harvesting research studies, organizing field cuttings, and assisting with research projects required the ability to be highly organized and able to manage multiple tasks at once. 

Horticultural knowledge: My work at the J.C. Raulston Arboretum provided me with a deep understanding of plant cultivation and care, including knowledge of different plant species, growing conditions, and plant maintenance. 

Teamwork and collaboration: As a member of a research team, gained valuable experience in working collaboratively with others to achieve common goals, and in communicating effectively with team members, supervisors, and stakeholders
